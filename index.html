Hidden side quest

Volos, the wildly overqualified cable chaos department.

This page is not part of the strategic analysis. It is a small backstage reward for anyone who clicked the almost invisible V and accepted that the most serious dashboard sometimes needs a deeply unserious secret room.

Open real Sonovia
Volos protocol
Actual public signal

Sonovia presents Lyris as a wireless DSP and app preset workflow for guitar and bass.

The public promise is simple: arrive, plug in, choose a preset, play. The serious version is faster, more reproducible setup. The meme version is that the cable drawer finally gets a performance review.

Timing

Lyris is framed as prototype validation with beta applications for 2026.

Officially: validation, musicians, feedback, sensible product learning. Unofficially: a polite calendar invite titled "please stop making setup take longer than the song".

Use case

Fewer pieces, faster setup, presets, live status and less stage friction.

The thesis is not complicated: musicians want to play, not negotiate with adapters. Volos exists for the exact moment where "just one more cable" becomes a personality trait.

Serious bit before the nonsense

What this page is allowed to claim

The factual part is deliberately narrow: Sonovia describes Lyris as a wireless DSP and app workflow for electric guitar and bass, with presets, receiver side processing and live status. Everything else on this hidden page is theatre, vibes and dashboard aftercare.

Actual problem Setup friction

Cables, tone repeatability, stage prep and the strange human tradition of troubleshooting five minutes before playing.

Actual product shape Wireless plus DSP plus app

A workflow layer for guitar and bass that tries to make sound setup more reproducible.

Actual status Prototype validation

Beta interest is positioned around 2026, which is also when many cable drawers will deny all allegations.
